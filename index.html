About

I'm a multidisciplinary engineer with a foundation in electrical engineering, software development, industrial automation, and applied AI. I moved to Canada in 2016 to study at the University of Toronto, and after graduating joined Siemens as a full-stack edge computing developer — connecting factory-floor equipment to digital systems so manufacturers could collect, analyze, and act on operational data.

Those years taught me that technology alone doesn't create value: data quality, domain expertise, and the ability to turn information into actionable insight are what separate success from failure. That insight led me to pursue a Master of Management Analytics at Rotman, and from there into GenAI — building RAG systems, agentic pipelines, and enterprise AI products. Today I'm excited by the intersection of AI, analytics, and software engineering: using technology not just to build systems, but to solve meaningful business problems and deliver measurable impact.

I'm naturally curious, enjoy building things end-to-end, and believe that the hardest part of engineering is rarely writing the code — it's communication, collaboration, and bridging the gap between technology and real-world context.
